<h1>Research</h1>

<p>
My research focuses on how ecological processes interact with human activities and social systems, particularly in aquatic environments in Asia. I combine ecological field surveys, statistical modeling, and interdisciplinary approaches linking natural and social sciences.
</p>

</section>



<section class="research-topic">

<h2>Market integration of invasive sailfin catfish</h2>

<figure class="research-figure">
<img src="{{ '/assets/img/research/market_sold_suckermouth.jpg' | relative_url }}">
<figcaption>
Sailfin catfish (Pterygoplichthys spp.) sold in a local market in Can Tho City, Mekong Delta, Vietnam.
</figcaption>
</figure>

<div class="research-description">

<p>
Invasive fishes are increasingly incorporated into local food systems, yet the landscape contexts and market mechanisms underlying this process remain poorly understood. We investigated the market occurrence and economic position of sailfin catfish (Pterygoplichthys spp.) in Can Tho City, Mekong Delta, Vietnam.
</p>

<p>
We surveyed 53 local retail markets in 2025, quantified sailfin catfish presence, and linked each market to surrounding land-use/land-cover (LULC) composition within a 1000-m radius.
</p>

<section class="research-topic">

<h2>Environmental DNA monitoring of river fish communities</h2>

<figure class="research-figure">
<img src="{{ '/assets/img/research/edna_sampling.jpg' | relative_url }}">
<figcaption>
Environmental DNA sampling in a river system to investigate spatial and temporal fish community dynamics.
</figcaption>
</figure>

<div class="research-description">

<p>
Environmental DNA (eDNA) metabarcoding provides a powerful method to monitor aquatic biodiversity across large spatial scales. Our work examines temporal beta diversity patterns in river systems and investigates how community replacement and nestedness components vary along environmental gradients.
</p>

<p>
Using year-round monitoring data, we analyze seasonal community dynamics and evaluate the ecological drivers shaping fish assemblages along river continua.
</p>
